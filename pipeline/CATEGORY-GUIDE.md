# Pub Quiz Category Guide

> **Gold standard.** This guide defines how categories should be named, scoped, and structured. The Category Agent uses it as its primary reference. Changes require human approval.
>
> Built from analysis of 42 categories (12 seeds + 30 generated), iterative testing of which categories produced good questions and which were deleted as unsuitable.

---

## 1. The Pub Test

Read the category name out loud as if you're a quizmaster announcing the next round. **Would the room nod and feel they have a shot, or would they groan?**

Good reactions: "Ooh, I'm good at this one" / "We've got a chance" / "This'll be fun"
Bad reactions: "What even is that?" / "Who cares?" / "That's not a pub quiz round"

---

## 2. Naming

### Rules
- **Use plain English.** "Classic Rock" not "Rock Music of the Classic Era". "Dog Breeds" not "Canine Taxonomy".
- **2-4 words ideal.** Short enough to fit on a round card, long enough to be specific.
- **Name it what a quizmaster would call it.** "James Bond Films" not "007 Franchise Filmography".
- **Avoid jargon, academic terms, and marketing speak.** "Beer and Brewing" not "Craft Beverage Production".

### Good Names
| Name | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Classic Rock | Everyone knows what this means |
| James Bond Films | Specific, fun, wide appeal |
| World Capitals | Clear scope, classic pub quiz |
| The Beatles | Iconic, everyone has an opinion |
| Premier League Football | Specific enough, broad enough |
| Dog Breeds | Fun, sociable, everyone can have a go |
| World War II | Major topic, endless good questions |

### Bad Names (deleted from our database)
| Name | Why It Failed |
|------|-------------|
| Human Anatomy | Feels like a medical exam |
| Social Media Platforms | Not interesting as trivia — too current, too niche |
| Cocktails and Mixology | "Mixology" is pretentious; "Cocktails" alone might work |
| Renaissance Art | Too narrow and academic for most pub tables |
| Ancient Civilizations | Vague and dry — "Ancient Egypt" or "Ancient Rome" would be better |
| The Victorian Era | Too narrow a time period for general knowledge |
| 90s Hip Hop | Too niche for a subcategory this early in the tree |

## 3. Scope

### The 20-Question Rule
A category must be broad enough that you could write **at least 20 questions that ordinary people might know the answers to**. If you struggle to think of 10, it's too narrow. If you could write 500, it might need subcategories.

### The Three-Person Rule
At a table of 6 random adults, **at least 3 should recognise the category and feel they could answer some questions in it**. If it's a topic only specialists care about, it's not a pub quiz category.

### Scope Spectrum
| Too Narrow | Just Right | Too Broad |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Beatles Albums 1963-1966 | The Beatles | Music |
| Quidditch Rules | Harry Potter | Fiction |
| French Impressionist Landscape Painting | Famous Paintings | Art |
| 2024 Premier League Season | Premier League Football | Sports |

## 4. Hierarchy

### Depth Rules
- **Depth 0 (root):** 12 seed themes. These are fixed: Science, History, Geography, Movies and TV, Sports, Art and Design, Music, Food and Drink, Nature and Animals, Technology, Literature, Gaming.
- **Depth 1 (child):** The sweet spot. Most subcategories live here. "The Beatles" under "Music", "James Bond Films" under "Movies and TV".
- **Depth 2 (grandchild):** Use sparingly. Only when a depth-1 category is genuinely large enough to subdivide. "Premier League Teams" under "Premier League Football" — maybe, once there are enough questions.
- **Depth 3 (great-grandchild):** Maximum allowed. Almost never needed. The tree shouldn't go this deep in practice.

### When to Create a Subcategory
- The parent has 50+ questions and a clear subtopic with its own identity
- The subtopic would be recognised as a standalone pub quiz round
- You can name it in 2-4 words without jargon

### When NOT to Create a Subcategory
- You're just adding an adjective to the parent ("Advanced Science", "Modern History")
- The subtopic is a single event or person (those are questions, not categories)
- Fewer than 3 people at a random table would recognise the topic
- It only exists because Wikipedia has an article about it

## 5. Category Types That Work Well

| Type | Examples | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| **Era-specific music** | 80s Pop, Classic Rock | People identify with decades |
| **Specific franchises** | James Bond, Marvel, Star Wars | Passionate fanbases, wide awareness |
| **Country/region geography** | African Geography, European Geography | Natural quiz round structure |
| **Specific sports** | Premier League, The Olympics | Casual fans still know plenty |
| **Food & drink subtypes** | Beer and Brewing, British Pub Food | Sociable, fun, everyone eats |
| **Famous people clusters** | US Presidents, British Monarchy | Well-known figures, lots of trivia |
| **Iconic works** | Shakespeare, Classic Disney Films | Cultural touchstones |
| **Animal groups** | Dog Breeds, Marine Life | People love animal questions |

---

## 6. Category Types That Don't Work

| Type | Examples | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| **Academic disciplines** | Human Anatomy, Quantum Physics | Feels like school |
| **Current tech/platforms** | Social Media Platforms, Cryptocurrency | Dates fast, niche audience |
| **Overly specific art movements** | Renaissance Art, Brutalist Architecture | Not enough general knowledge |
| **Niche hobbies** | Cocktail Mixology, Board Game Mechanics | Specialist interest only |
| **Vague historical periods** | The Victorian Era, Bronze Age | Too broad or too narrow depending on region |
| **Genre-only categories** | Horror Films, Romantic Comedies | Better as a subcategory with a more specific angle |

---

## 7. Balancing the Tree

### Current Gaps to Fill
When proposing new categories, look for gaps:
- **Underrepresented roots:** If "Technology" has 0 good subcategories and "Music" has 4, propose tech subcategories
- **Missing crowd-pleasers:** If there's no "Harry Potter", "Friends (TV Show)", or "Disney" yet, those are obvious wins
- **Regional balance:** Don't make everything UK/US-centric. "Bollywood", "African Geography", "Japanese Food" add variety

### Avoid Clustering
Don't propose 3 music subcategories in one batch when there are roots with no children. Spread proposals across the tree.

---

## 8. Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Example | Fix |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| Adding "and" to combine two topics | "Cars and Motorcycles" | Pick one or use the parent |
| Using the parent name with an adjective | "Modern History" | Be specific: "World War II" |
| Creating a category for one question | "The Moon Landing" | That's a question, not a category |
| Jargon in the name | "Herpetology" | "Reptiles and Amphibians" |
| Trendy/ephemeral topics | "TikTok Trends" | Will be irrelevant in 2 years |
| Categories that need a qualifier | "Classical Music (not Classic Rock)" | Confusing — rename to avoid ambiguity |
